## Commitment of Traders (COT) Trading Strategy

### Overview

The Commitment of Traders (COT) report is published weekly by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC). It shows how different types of traders are positioned in the futures markets. Understanding COT data can give traders insight into market sentiment and potential future price movements.

### Key Concepts

#### Market Participants

The COT report categorizes traders into three main groups:

1. **Commercial Traders (Smart Money)**
   - Large hedgers (producers, merchants, processors)
   - Have a real business need for the commodity
   - Typically correct at market extremes
   - Use futures to hedge their business risk

2. **Non-Commercial Traders (Large Speculators)**
   - Large speculators (hedge funds, large traders)
   - Trade for profit based on price movements
   - Tend to be wrong at market extremes
   - Often follow trends rather than anticipate reversals

3. **Non-Reportable Traders (Small Speculators)**
   - Small retail traders
   - Generally follow the crowd
   - Less sophisticated analysis

### Step-by-Step COT Trading Strategy

#### Step 1: Identify Extreme Positions

- Look for historically high/low net positions of commercials or large specs
- Use multi-year extremes (5-year, 10-year highs/lows)
- Example: If commercials are net long at a 5-year high, expect bullish momentum
- Extreme readings often signal potential reversals

#### Step 2: Confirm with Price Action

- If commercials are extreme but price hasn't moved yet → Potential reversal soon
- If price is already reversing in line with COT → Strong confirmation
- Look for divergence between price and COT positions
- Wait for price pattern confirmation (pin bar, breakout, etc.)

#### Step 3: Trade Setup Rules

##### A) Fading Large Speculators (Contrarian Trade)

- **When large specs are extremely long** → Look for short opportunities
- **When large specs are extremely short** → Look for long opportunities
- **Why?** Large specs tend to be wrong at extremes
- They often crowd into one side of the market, creating overextension

##### B) Following Commercials (Smart Money Trade)

- **If commercials are heavily long** → Consider buying dips
- **If commercials are heavily short** → Consider selling rallies
- **Why?** Commercials hedge real business needs (they know true value)
- They have superior knowledge of supply/demand fundamentals

#### Step 4: Entry & Risk Management

**Entry:**
- Wait for a price pattern confirmation (e.g., pin bar, breakout)
- Use technical analysis for entry timing
- Don't enter solely based on COT extremes

**Stop Loss:**
- Beyond recent swing high/low
- Give price room to breathe
- Typical stop: 1-3 ATR (Average True Range)

**Take Profit:**
- At next key support/resistance level
- Use Fibonacci extensions for targets
- Trail stops as price moves in your favor

### COT Trading Indicators

| Indicator | Signal | Interpretation |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Commercial Net Position | High/Low | Smart money sentiment |
| Non-Commercial Net Position | High/Low | Speculator positioning |
| Commercial/Large Spec Ratio | Above/Below Average | Contrarian signal |
| Open Interest | Rising/Falling | Market participation |

### Example Trade Using COT Data

**Scenario:**
- EUR/USD: Commercials are net long at a 2-year high
- Price is consolidating near support
- Large specs are at extreme short positions

**Trade Setup:**
1. Wait for price to reject support level
2. Look for bullish candlestick pattern
3. Enter long on confirmation
4. Target: Previous highs / resistance level
5. Stop: Below recent swing low

### COT Data Sources

- **Primary:** CFTC.gov (weekly reports)
- **Commercial Services:** Barchart.com, Quandl
- **COT Charts:** Commitmenttraders.com, Finviz

### Limitations of COT

1. **Lagging Data:** Reports are delayed by 3 days
2. **Position Changes:** Large traders can exit before price moves
3. **Interpretation:** High/low readings can persist
4. **Market Changes:** Structure changes over time
