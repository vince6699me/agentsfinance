# Fundamental Calendar Skill

Economic releases drive short-term volatility. Trading around news requires careful timing.

## Impact Levels

| Level | Examples | Volatility | Action |
|-------|----------|------------|--------|
| **High** | NFP, CPI, FOMC, GDP | Very High | Avoid/fade |
| **Medium** | Retail Sales, PMI | Moderate | Trade carefully |
| **Low** | Trade Balance, Housing | Mild | Normal trading |

## High-Impact Releases

| Release | Frequency | Typical Move |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| NFP (US) | Monthly (1st Fri) | 50-150 pips |
| CPI (US) | Monthly | 30-100 pips |
| FOMC Rate Decision | 8x/year | 50-200 pips |
| GDP (US) | Quarterly | 40-100 pips |
| ECB Rate Decision | 8x/year | 40-150 pips |
| BoE Rate Decision | 8x/year | 50-150 pips |

## Trading Rules

### Before High-Impact News
1. **Close positions 30 min before** major releases
2. **Avoid new entries** 15 min before
3. **Widen stops** if holding through news
4. **Reduce position size** by 50%

### After High-Impact News
1. **Wait 15-30 min** for initial spike to settle
2. **Trade the reaction** to the data vs expectations
3. **Favor the trend** if data confirms existing trend
4. **Fade the spike** if overreaction is clear

### Contango/Rollover Events
- Avoid holding positions through major events
- Check economic calendar daily
- Mark high-impact dates on charts

## Confidence Scoring

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Release vs expectations (big miss/beat) | +30% |
| Multiple confirms from related releases | +25% |
| Central bank reaction aligned | +20% |
| Historical reaction pattern | +15% |
| Current trend confirmation | +10% |
