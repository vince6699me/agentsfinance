# News Events Skill

Scheduled events create predictable volatility windows.

## Event Categories

### Central Bank Events (Highest Impact)
| Event | Frequency | Volatility |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| Fed FOMC Rate Decision | 8x/year | Extreme |
| ECB Rate Decision | 8x/year | Very High |
| BoE Rate Decision | 8x/year | Very High |
| Fed Chair Speech | As needed | High |
| ECB President Press Conf | After each meeting | High |

### Economic Data (High Impact)
| Event | Frequency | Volatility |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| NFP (US) | Monthly | Very High |
| CPI | Monthly | High |
| GDP | Quarterly | High |
| PMI | Monthly | Medium-High |
| Retail Sales | Monthly | Medium |

### Geopolitical Events (Unpredictable)
- Elections, referendums
- Trade negotiations
- Sanctions, wars
- Natural disasters

## Pre-Event Trading

1. **Check calendar daily**
2. **Mark high-impact dates on charts**
3. **Reduce exposure before events**
4. **Widen stops or close before**

## Post-Event Trading

### Volatility Breakout
```
Event → Large candle in direction → Wait for retest → Enter
Stop: Retest low/high ± ATR
TP: 2x risk
```

### Fade the Move
```
Event → Large spike → Price reverses → Enter opposite
Stop: Beyond spike extreme
TP: Retest of pre-event level
```

## Confidence Scoring

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Actual vs forecast surprise | +30% |
| Market's prior positioning | +20% |
| Subsequent data confirms | +20% |
| Volatility spike vs historical | +15% |
| Analyst reaction consensus | +15% |
